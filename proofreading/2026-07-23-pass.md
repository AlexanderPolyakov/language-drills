## Findings & fixes — 4 items across 4 files

The corpus was very clean this pass (accents, elisions, agreement,
answer-in-options, cue/answer structure all verified across 50 exercises). The
genuine defects were two near-duplicate pairs and one BrE spelling / register
slip.

### English
| File | Item | Category | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| `adverbs-frequency-a2.json` | "I {1} drink **soda**…" | near-duplicate + register | Removed. Exact twin of "I {1} drink **fizzy drinks**; I prefer water (about 10%)." → `rarely` (same frame, same %, same answer); "soda" is also American vs. the app's British register. Kept the "fizzy drinks" copy. |
| `vocabulary-c1.json` | "…to {1} a complex issue into simple terms." | spelling (BrE) | `distill` → `distil` (British single-l) in both the option and the answer, and `To distill` → `To distil` in the explanation. Answer still present verbatim in options. |

### French
| File | Item | Category | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| `possessive-adjectives-a1.json` | "Avez-vous reçu {1} **paquet** ce matin ?" | near-duplicate | Removed. Same sentence frame as "Avez-vous reçu {1} **colis** ce matin ?" → `votre` (synonyms, same lesson point). Kept the `colis` copy, whose explanation (invariable noun) is the more instructive of the two. |
| `reflexive-verbs-a2.json` | "Je {1} dès que le réveil sonne." | near-duplicate | Removed. Same conjugation task as item 0 "Le matin, je {1} à sept heures." — both cue `se lever` / `je` / answer `me lève`. Kept item 0. |

Low-severity notes deliberately **not** changed (design-inherent or valid
variants, not defects): context-free possessive-pronoun items where a distractor
is also grammatical (`possessive-adjectives-a1` EN); Oxford `-ize` spellings in
`vocabulary-b2`/`vocabulary-c1` (valid British); `passive-voice-b1` items that
extend slightly beyond the lesson blurb; `passe-simple-c1` verb-pair repetition
(every sentence distinct and correct).
