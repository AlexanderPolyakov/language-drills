# Proof-reading pass — 2026-07-21

Automated proof-reading routine. One agent per language, each given 25 randomly
selected exercises to review for both **content** (language/factual correctness)
and **structure** (schema + engine compatibility).

## Scope

- **English:** 25 random exercises reviewed.
- **French:** 25 random exercises reviewed.
- Structural checks per file: `{n}` placeholder count == `blanks.length`
  (contiguous 1..N), cloze `answer ∈ options`, no duplicate options, every item
  has an `explanation`, fill blanks have a `cue`, transforms have an `answer`,
  valid JSON, intact accents, no duplicate item texts.

The dominant defect this pass was **left-over duplicates** from content-automation
runs: several files carried an exact or near-duplicate of an item alongside a
later "fixed" copy (ambiguity removed, spelling normalised, or answer list
completed). In each case the superseded copy was removed and the better twin
kept.

## Findings & fixes (25 items removed, 11 items/fields edited across 16 files)

### English — deletions (16 items)

- **`conditionals.json`** — removed 2 items. `If you heat ice, it {1}.` and
  `If you {1} water to 100°C, it {2}.` each appeared twice; the originals also
  listed `will melt` / `will boil` as distractors, which are *grammatically
  correct* first-conditional readings — ambiguous. Kept the fixed twins (zero
  conditional only).
- **`modals.json`** — removed 2 items. Exact duplicate of `I {1} get up early
  tomorrow…` (kept the compact copy); and the near-duplicate of the
  `drive carefully / headlights / speed limit` item that lacked the
  disambiguating `because the law requires it` clause (kept the clearer copy).
- **`nuanced-modality-c2.json`** — removed 1 exact duplicate of
  `There's the doorbell. {1} the courier…` (kept the copy with the cleaner
  distractor set).
- **`inversion-c1.json`** — removed 1 duplicate of the `Not only…` transform
  (identical prompt, answer array merely reordered).
- **`vocabulary-b2.json`** — removed 4 items. British-spelling re-issues
  (`criticised`, `jeopardise`, `summarise`, `coordinate`/`organise`) were
  appended on top of the American originals, so the file both tested each word
  twice and mixed `-ise`/`-ize`. Kept the American set for internal consistency.
- **`vocabulary-verbs-a2.json`** — removed 2 items. Exact duplicate of
  `Please {1} this letter at the post office.`; and `I forgot to {1} my
  brother's birthday…` → `remember`, where "forgot to remember" is illogical
  (a later item, `I always try to {1} my brother's birthday.`, supersedes it).
- **`possessive-adjectives-a1.json`** — removed 2 items. `The baby dropped {1}
  toy…` whose explanation ("the baby as a thing/animal-like reference") is poor
  A1 pedagogy — the near-identical `The kitten dropped {1} toy…` teaches the
  same `its` naturally; and the `new flat` near-duplicate of `new apartment`.
- **`vocabulary-adjectives-a2.json`** — removed 1 near-duplicate
  (`shopping centre` vs `shopping mall`, both → `busy`).
- **`vocabulary-nouns-a1.json`** — removed 1 wordier near-duplicate of the
  `sister` definition item.

### English — edits (10 items/fields)

- **`mixed-conditionals-b2.json`** — items 20–27 embedded the cue inline in the
  carrier text as `{1} (book)`, `{1} (not tell)`, … The `fill` engine already
  renders `blanks[].cue` in parentheses next to the input, so the cue displayed
  **twice**. Stripped the inline `(cue)` from all 8 texts (matching items 0–19).
- **`phrasal-verbs.json`** — the `filled in the form` blank listed `filled out`
  as a wrong distractor, but "fill out a form" is fully correct English, so two
  options were right. Replaced the `filled out` distractor with a clearly-wrong
  `filled off`.
- **`passive-voice-b1.json`** — title "Passive voice: present and past simple"
  understated the content (items also cover modal, continuous and perfect
  passives). Broadened to "Passive voice: common forms".

### French — deletions (4 items)

- **`nominalisation-c1.json`** — removed 1 of a duplicated `rompre → rupture`
  transform; the deleted copy was *also* mislabelled ("par un déverbal SANS
  suffixe", though `rupture` carries the `-ure` suffix — its own explanation
  said so). Kept the correctly-labelled `« -ure »` copy.
- **`subject-pronouns-a1.json`** — removed 1 exact duplicate of `Je {1} content.`
  (→ `suis`).
- **`vocabulary-adjectives-a1.json`** — removed 2 incomplete duplicates:
  `Je suis {1} de te voir.` (accepted only `content`/`heureux`) and
  `Je suis très {1} ce matin.` (accepted only `fatigué`). Since `je` is not
  gender-marked here, the feminine forms are equally correct; kept the later
  copies whose answer lists already include `contente`/`heureuse` and `fatiguée`.

### French — edits (1 item)

- **`partitive-articles-a2.json`** — `Tu as besoin {1} argent…` → `de l'` was
  mis-framed: `avoir besoin` governs bare `de` (`besoin d'argent`), not a
  partitive article, and `d'argent` was not even an option. Reframed the carrier
  to `Tu as {1} argent…`, where the partitive `de l'` genuinely applies (another
  item, `Tu dépenses {1} argent…`, already covers this word correctly).
