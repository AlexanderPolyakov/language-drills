## English pass — VERDICT: all 5 OK, no edits

- **it-cleft `It was not until the following week that …`** — OK. `that` (not
  `when`) correctly completes `It was not until X …`; main verb stays
  affirmative because the negation sits in `not until`.
- **wh-cleft `What I object to is his tone, not his argument`** — OK. Valid
  preposition-stranding pseudo-cleft; both corrective tags (`not` /
  `rather than`) idiomatic.
- **`The reason (why) … was that …`** — OK. All three variants acceptable;
  complement is `that`, not `because`.
- **cloze `Hardly had she sat down {when} the telephone rang again`** — OK.
  Blank 1: only `Hardly` works with a `when` completer (`No sooner` pairs with
  `than`; `Much as`/`Given that` don't license the inversion). Blank 2: `when`
  is the sole correct correlative of `Hardly`; `than`/`that`/`as` all fail.
- **cloze `Notwithstanding the committee's repeated objections, …`** — OK.
  `Seeing that`/`Insofar as` require a clause (verbless fragment before a bare
  NP); `Owing to` is grammatical but wrong by meaning (cause vs concession). No
  second correct answer.

## French pass — VERDICT: all 5 OK, 1 cosmetic dedupe

- **`Rends-le-moi !`** — OK. Affirmative imperative order verb-COD-COI with
  hyphens; `me` → tonic `moi` → `Rends-le-moi` (not `Rends-moi-le`).
- **`L'avocate les lui a montrées.`** — OK. Two 3rd-person pronouns → direct
  first (`les lui`); participle agrees with preceding COD `les` (fem. pl.) →
  `montrées`.
- **`Elle l'y a inscrite.`** — OK. `la` → `l'` before `y`; order `l'y`;
  participle `inscrite` (fem. sg.). Cosmetic: a duplicated answer variant was
  removed (kept with-period / without-period only).
- **nominalisation `arriver → l'arrivée tardive des secours …`** — OK. Feminine
  `-ée` deverbal on the past participle; subject → `de` complement; adverb →
  adjective `tardive`.
- **nominalisation `lent → la lenteur désespérante …`** — OK. Feminine `-eur`
  quality noun from adjective; `désespérante` (active sense) correctly chosen
  over `désespérée`.
